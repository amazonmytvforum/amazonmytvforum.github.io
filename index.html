Complete Guide to amazon.com/mytv Activation

amazon.com/mytv is the dedicated device activation page provided by Amazon for linking Smart TVs, streaming sticks, and gaming consoles to your Prime Video account. Whether you own a Fire TV Stick, a Roku device, an LG Smart TV, or a PlayStation console, the activation process always begins the same way — by visiting amazon.com/mytv.

Why Do You Need to Activate at amazon.com/mytv?

When you install Amazon Prime Video on a new device, Amazon needs to verify your identity securely. Instead of entering your full password on a TV remote (which is slow and error-prone), Amazon generates a short 6-digit code on your TV and asks you to enter it on a phone or computer at amazon.com/mytv. This makes activation fast, secure, and simple.

Common Issues with amazon.com/mytv and How to Fix Them

The most common problem users face is an expired code. Since codes generated for amazon.com/mytv are valid only for a short window (usually 10–15 minutes), make sure you enter the code promptly. If the code has expired, simply restart the Prime Video app to get a new one.

Another issue can be signing in to the wrong Amazon account. If you have multiple accounts, ensure you are logged into the correct one at amazon.com/mytv before entering your code.

Using amazon.com/mytv Outside the US

The amazon.com/mytv page is globally accessible. Depending on your country, you may be redirected to your local Amazon store (e.g., amazon.co.uk/mytv for the UK, amazon.in/mytv for India). The activation steps remain the same across all regions.
