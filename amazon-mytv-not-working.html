Quick Fix Checklist

Before diving deep, try these quick fixes first — they solve 80% of amazon.com/mytv problems.

🔧 Quick Fixes — Check Each One

Code is still visible on TV (not expired) — codes expire in ~10 minutes
You're visiting the real amazon.com/mytv (not a fake lookalike site)
You're logged into the correct Amazon account (the one with Prime)
Your internet connection is working on both TV and phone/computer
You tried a different browser or used incognito/private mode
You restarted the Prime Video app on your TV to get a fresh code

Fix Every amazon.com/mytv Error

Find your specific error below and follow the fix steps.

⏰
Error: "Code Expired" or "Invalid Code" Most Common

Amazon MyTV codes expire after 10–15 minutes. If you waited too long, the code is no longer valid.

1
On your TV, close the Amazon Prime Video app completely.
2
Reopen the app — a new 6-digit code will appear on screen.
3
Immediately open amazon.com/mytv on your phone or computer.
4
Enter the new code right away. Do not wait.
📄
Error: Blank White Screen at amazon.com/mytv

A blank page usually means browser cache or cookie conflict.

1
Open your browser in Incognito / Private mode.
2
Go to amazon.com/mytv in the incognito window.
3
If it loads, clear your regular browser's cache and cookies and try again normally.
4
Alternatively, try a completely different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
🔐
Error: Can't Sign In / Wrong Account

You must sign into the Amazon account linked to your Prime Video subscription.

1
On amazon.com/mytv, click "Sign Out" if you are logged in.
2
Sign in again using the correct email and password for your Prime account.
3
Then enter your TV's activation code.
📺
Error: "This Device Is Already Registered"

Your TV is already linked to an Amazon account — possibly under a different email.

1
Go to amazon.com → Account → Manage Your Content and Devices.
2
Under "Devices" tab, find and deregister the old device.
3
Restart the Prime Video app on your TV to get a fresh code.
4
Re-activate using amazon.com/mytv with the new code.
🌐
Error: Page Won't Load / Connection Error

Amazon's servers may be temporarily down, or your internet connection may have issues.

1
Check if other websites load normally in your browser.
2
Visit downdetector.com to see if Amazon is having outages.
3
Restart your router and try again after 2–3 minutes.
4
Try using mobile data instead of WiFi (or vice versa).

FAQ – amazon.com/mytv Not Working

Double-check that you're entering all 6 digits correctly. Codes are numbers only — no letters. Also make sure you haven't waited too long; expired codes always fail. Get a fresh code from your TV and enter it immediately.
Switch from WiFi to mobile data (or vice versa) and try again. Also clear your phone browser's cache. If using the Amazon app, try the browser instead. Safari on iPhone and Chrome on Android both work well with amazon.com/mytv.
If amazon.com/mytv is down, you can sometimes sign in directly on the TV using your Amazon email and password (though this is harder on a TV remote). You can also try the Amazon app on your phone — some devices support in-app activation.
